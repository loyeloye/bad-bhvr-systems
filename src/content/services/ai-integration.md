## Practical AI, not AI theatre

Most businesses that try to "implement AI" end up with a chatbot that answers three questions badly and sits unused after two months.

Practical AI integration looks different. It starts with a real problem your team has — a task that takes too long, a question that gets asked repeatedly, a document that needs to be processed faster — and works backwards to find where AI can actually help.

## Where AI earns its place in your business

**Customer response and triage**
Train an AI assistant on your products, services, pricing, and FAQs. Let it handle the 70% of inquiries that are routine, and route the remaining 30% to the right person — with context already populated.

**Document generation**
Contracts, proposals, reports, summaries — AI can draft these in seconds from structured inputs. Your team reviews and approves; the drafting time drops from hours to minutes.

**Lead qualification**
AI can read an inbound inquiry and score it: urgency, fit, intent. The hot leads get flagged and escalated immediately. The cold ones enter a nurture sequence. No one manually reads every email.

**Internal knowledge tools**
Build an internal assistant that knows your SOPs, your client history, your product specs. Your team asks it questions instead of digging through folders or pinging colleagues.

**Data extraction from documents**
Process invoices, applications, or submissions at scale — AI pulls the structured data out of unstructured documents and puts it where it belongs.

## How we approach it

We don't recommend AI for things it's not good at. Every AI feature we build goes through a clear ROI question: is this saving meaningful time or improving a meaningful outcome? If the answer isn't yes, we won't build it.
