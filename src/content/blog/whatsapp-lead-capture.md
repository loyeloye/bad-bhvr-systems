If your business runs on WhatsApp — and most Nigerian businesses do — you're probably losing more revenue than you realise.

Not because you're not responding. Not because your product isn't good. Because every time someone messages you and doesn't hear back fast enough, or messages you and gets replied to but never followed up on again, you've lost a deal you never officially lost.

## The invisible leak

Here's what the typical WhatsApp sales funnel looks like in practice:

1. Someone sees your ad or gets a referral
2. They WhatsApp you
3. You (or a team member) reply, or don't, depending on whether someone saw it
4. A back-and-forth happens, or doesn't
5. The conversation goes cold
6. That person either buys from a competitor or follows up weeks later when you've forgotten the context

The problem isn't effort. It's infrastructure. There's no system holding the conversation together.

## What a proper system looks like

The fix isn't complicated — it's just not something most businesses have set up:

**Step 1: Every inbound WhatsApp message creates a CRM record.** The moment someone messages you, their number, their message, and the time are captured in your CRM. Automatically. You never lose a contact again.

**Step 2: Contacts get tagged.** Based on what they say, they're tagged — new inquiry, returning customer, price question, complaint. This happens automatically using keyword detection or, for more nuance, a simple AI classification step.

**Step 3: Follow-up is triggered.** If no one has replied in two hours, an automated message goes out acknowledging the inquiry and setting a response expectation. If a conversation went cold after three days, a re-engagement message fires.

**Step 4: Your team has a pipeline.** Instead of scrolling through chat history to remember who said what, your CRM shows every open inquiry, its status, and who's handling it.

## What this is worth

Think about your current monthly WhatsApp inquiry volume. Now estimate: what percentage of those convert? What if that number went up by 20% simply because nothing fell through the cracks?

For most businesses, that's a meaningful number. Often bigger than whatever they spend on ads.

## Building it

The tools for this are available and not particularly expensive:

- **WhatsApp Business API** (through providers like 360dialog or Twilio) gives you programmatic access to your WhatsApp
- **n8n or a similar workflow tool** handles the automation logic
- **Zoho CRM or any modern CRM** holds the contact and conversation history

The integration layer is where most businesses get stuck — not because it's technically hard, but because someone needs to design the logic, connect the tools, and test it properly.
